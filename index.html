Mission Briefing: Survival & Escape
Objective

You are stranded on Mars. Your goal is to survive the harsh environment, manage your resources, and build a rover to escape before it's too late.

Survival (The Sol Cycle)
  • Time: Each Sol (day) has 24 hours. Actions like moving, mining, and building consume time.
  • Resources: You must manage your Food and Water rations. Consuming too little will damage your health.
  • Shelter: You must be in a shelter at the end of your turn to avoid massive health and morale loss from the Mars elements.
  • Morale: Low morale wastes time as you wallow in self-pity. Keep it high to remain efficient.
Buildings & Research
  • Wells, Farms, Mines: Essential for gathering Water, Food, and Stone.
  • Labs: The key to escape. Build a Lab to research and construct Rover Parts (Frame, Engine, Wheels) and advanced tools.
  • Upgrades: Buildings can be upgraded to increase their efficiency and provide better protection.
Winning the Game
  1. Construct all Rover Parts: Use the Lab to build the Frame, Engine, and Wheels.
  2. Launch: Once your rover is ready, initiate the final escape.
  3. The Final Stand: Survive the combat phase by defending your ship from Martian threats. If your ship survives, you win!
Controls
WASD - Move & Jump
SPACE - Attack
Mouse - Interact with Map
Sliders - Set Rations
How You Can Lose
  • Death: If your Health reaches 0, you die and the mission fails.
  • Starvation/Dehydration: Setting your rations too low will slowly drain your health each Sol.
  • Exposure: Ending a turn outside of a shelter will cause massive damage to health and morale.
  • Final Game Failure: If your escape rover is destroyed by Martian threats during the launch phase, you lose.
Pro-Tip

Use Propaganda to sabotage other players. Placing it on their buildings increases the number of enemies they face in the final stand!
